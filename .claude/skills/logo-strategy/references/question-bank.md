# Strategy Workshop Question Bank

Cover every area below before generating ideas. Don't ask all at once — group into 2–4 question rounds, lead with the highest-leverage areas (Offering, Audience, Perception, Positioning), and infer/confirm the rest. Skip a question whose answer is already obvious from context.

## 1. Offering & category
- What does the brand make, sell, or do — in one plain sentence?
- What category does it compete in? Who are the 2–3 main competitors?
- What does it do better or differently than them?

## 2. Audience
- Who is the primary customer? (demographics, but more importantly mindset/values)
- Is there a secondary or future audience to grow into?
- What does the audience care about when choosing in this category?

## 3. Perception & personality
- Pick 3–5 adjectives for how the brand should feel (e.g. premium, rugged, playful, clinical, warm, technical).
- What feeling should it NEVER give off?
- If the brand were a person/celebrity/car, who/what would it be?

## 4. Positioning
- Premium, mid-market, or value? Mass or niche?
- Is the brand expanding out of a niche into a wider market? (changes how broad the concept must be)
- What's the core promise to the customer?

## 5. Values & story
- What does the brand stand for / believe in?
- Origin story or founder reason-for-being, if any?
- One word the brand wants to own in the customer's mind?

## 6. Hard constraints
- Existing name to use, or is naming open?
- Colors, symbols, or styles that must be kept or must be avoided?
- Where will the logo appear? (app icon, embroidery, signage, packaging, favicon, vehicle livery — drives simplicity/scalability needs)
- Mono/one-color reproduction required?
- Any cultural, legal, or trademark sensitivities?

## 7. Preferences (capture, then hold loosely)
- Logos the user loves or hates, and why? (in or out of category)
- Wordmark vs. symbol preference?
- Any literal imagery they're drawn to — and is it cliché for the category? (steer away from category clichés)

## Synthesis check
Before leaving the workshop, confirm you can state, with conviction:
- the single sentence of what they sell,
- the audience,
- the 3–5 personality adjectives,
- the positioning,
- the perception goal,
- at least one strong candidate for the **centralized concept**.

If any is shaky, ask one more targeted round.
